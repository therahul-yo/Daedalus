The oldest design document

Three ways to cross the Aegean

Daedalus built wings of wax and feathers, and gave his son a single engineering constraint: fly the middle course. Three thousand years later, it is still the correct thermal policy.

I

Icarus flew high

Full-power prefill, no pacing — every other MLX engine. The sun melts the wax: your Air pins at HEAVY thermal pressure inside the first prompt, clocks throttle, and throughput decays with every round. It never recovers mid-session.

II

Too low drowns

Overcautious throttling wastes the sky. OpenCode kills any request at 300 seconds, streamed or not — timidity without a cache is just a slower crash into the sea.

III

Daedalus arrived

Race while cool. Ease off as live thermal pressure rises. Never re-fly a league already flown. Cool silicon runs faster when it runs — the middle path is the fast path.

Τέχνη

Four inventions, one workshop

Built on mlx-lm's public primitives — no monkey-patching, no forks to chase.

The Labyrinth

A prefix cache that survives everything

Your agent's giant system prompt is prefilled once, ever — persisted to disk as KV state and recalled exactly on every later turn, across crashes and reboots. Correct even for hybrid-attention models (Qwen3.5, Gemma 4) where stock servers silently miss every time.

The Thread

Resumable prefill

Checkpoints every 4k tokens. A timeout, a crash, or a client's hard deadline never sends you back to the entrance — the retry picks up the thread exactly where it dropped.

The Middle Path

The first thermal governor in local inference

Reads macOS's five-level thermal-pressure signal live, no sudo — including the two levels Apple's public API hides — and duty-cycles prefill against it. Three tempers: performance · balanced · cool.

The Herald

Keepalives and tool calls that never hang

SSE heartbeats carry progress, thermal state, and ETA from the first second of a long prefill. Tool calls stream as strict OpenAI deltas — indexed, never empty — so pi, OpenCode, and every OpenAI-compatible agent simply work.
